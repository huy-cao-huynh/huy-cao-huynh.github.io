I'm a 3rd year undergraduate student at University of Washington. I'm broadly interested in how foundation models can become better reasoners by overcoming context limitations and intelligently integrating external knowledge. This interest spans retrieval-augmented generation and agentic tool use as mechanisms to mitigate context window degradation and catastrophic forgetting, united by the question of what models can learn to do versus what they simply know.

## Research

I'm currently a researcher at the [UW Graphics and Imaging Laboratory](https://grail.cs.washington.edu/) under the mentorship of PhD student [Jingwei Ma](https://jingweim.github.io/), and advised by Prof. [Steve Seitz](https://www.smseitz.com/), Prof. [Ira Kemelmacher-Shlizerman](https://www.irakemelmacher.com/), and Prof. [Brian Curless](https://homes.cs.washington.edu/~curless/). My work focuses on reference-based super-resolution tasks, leveraging generative models.

I'm also an undergraduate researcher at the [UW Systems Lab](https://syslab.cs.washington.edu/) under the mentorship of PhD student [Tapan Chugh](https://chughtapan.github.io/), and advised by [Arvind Krishnamurthy](https://sites.google.com/cs.washington.edu/arvind). My work focuses on defining and identifying web agent system failures using LLM-as-a-judge.
